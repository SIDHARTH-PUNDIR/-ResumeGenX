The Writer's Guide (For the End User)
The Resume Definition Language (.rdl) is designed to let you focus on your content, not formatting. It behaves like a standard notepad. You do not need to know how to code, and you do not need to use programming syntax like quotation marks or brackets.

Just follow these 5 simple formatting rules:

1. Assigning Information
Define your personal details using either an equals sign (=) or a colon (:). The compiler treats them exactly the same, so use whichever feels natural.

Phone = +91-XXXXXXXXXX

Email : user@example.com

2. Organizing Sections
Structure your resume using the Section and SubSection keywords. This tells the compiler how to group your experiences.

Section: Experience

    SubSection = Microsoft

3. Writing Descriptions (No Quotes Needed)
When writing descriptions, just type normally. Do not wrap your sentences in quotation marks. The compiler is smart enough to understand that commas, semicolons, and other punctuation are just part of your sentence.

Summary = Passionate developer; specialized in compilers, networking, and databases.

4. Multi-Line Paragraphs
If you write a long paragraph and want to break it across multiple lines so it is easier to read in your editor, just press Enter and Indent (press Tab or Space) on the next line. The compiler will automatically stitch the lines together into one continuous paragraph on the final PDF.

Description = Built a fast, secure system.
    It utilized an optimized database structure. (This will print as one continuous sentence)

5. Bullet Points
To create a list of achievements under a job or project, drop down to a new line, indent, and type a hyphen (-).

Highlights:
    - Designed the core architecture for the system.
    - Implemented high concurrency data handling.
